## Risk: Insufficient HARA Coverage Leading to Undetected Hazardous Failures

If the hazard and risk analysis (HARA) does not cover all operational scenarios (e.g., field, forest, street environments; 24/7 duty cycles; full lifecycle including maintenance), safety-critical fault conditions may go undetected, resulting in hazardous machine behavior that violates the targeted SIL/PL level and applicable norms (IEC 61508, ISO 26262). Mitigation requires a structured HARA review covering all use environments and lifecycle phases, with formal sign-off before SIL/PL targets are locked.
